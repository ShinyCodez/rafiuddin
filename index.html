About Me

Hi! I'm Rafi Uddin, a Human Biology major at Hunter College (CUNY) pursuing a career in healthcare with a strong interest in research, technology, and STEM education.

I currently work as an Undergraduate Research Assistant at Weill Cornell Medicine, where I study myeloid stem cells and immune–nervous system interactions using techniques such as cell culture, RT-qPCR, flow cytometry, ELISA, western blotting, and microscopy.

My background combines biology, physics, computer science, and leadership. I’ve co-authored a machine learning paper submitted to NeurIPS, published a physics textbook, and led large STEM organizations focused on expanding access to science and technology.
